Tsadimas Anargyros was born in Lamia on October 22, 1979. He currently serves as an Assistant Professor at the Department of Informatics and Computer Engineering of the University of West Attica. He holds a PhD in Information Systems from Harokopio University of Athens, focusing on the model-based design of Enterprise Information Systems using SysML. He earned his BSc in Applied Informatics from the University of Macedonia in 2002 and an MSc in Advanced Information Systems from the National and Kapodistrian University of Athens in 2005.

His academic career at Harokopio University spanned several pivotal roles, beginning in 2007 as an instructor (under PD 407/80). During the period of 2007–2016, he was responsible for the installation, configuration, and monitoring of the Department of Informatics and Telematics' datacenter, laboratory equipment, and network services. In March 2016, he was appointed as a member of the Special Technical Laboratory Staff (E.T.E.P.), and in January 2020, he progressed to the rank of Laboratory Teaching Staff (E.DI.P.). His formal subject area is defined as "Design, Development, and Operation of Complex Computing Systems".

Beyond his academic tenure, he possesses significant industry experience in the startup ecosystem as a Software Engineer, DevOps Engineer, and Technology Advisor. His research interests include DevOps methodologies, distributed systems, and enterprise information systems engineering. He has taught a wide range of courses, including Operating Systems, Distributed Systems, System Analysis, and Object-Oriented Programming, and has contributed to numerous R&D projects funded by the European Union and the Greek State.
